Item 1A. Risk Factors

The Company's business, reputation, results of operations, financial condition and stock price can be affected by a number of factors, whether currently known or unknown, including those described below. When any one or more of these risks materialize from time to time, the Company's business, reputation, results of operations, financial condition and stock price can be materially adversely affected.

The COVID-19 pandemic has adversely affected the Company's business and results of operations and could continue to do so in the future. The pandemic may also adversely affect the broader economy, which could adversely impact demand for the Company's products and services.

Global and regional economic conditions could materially adversely affect the Company. The Company has international operations with sales outside the U.S. representing a majority of the Company's total net sales.

The technology industry is subject to rapid innovation and shifts in consumer demand. The Company may be unable to compete successfully against current and future competitors.

Cybersecurity threats and data breaches could materially adversely affect the Company's business. The Company collects and stores significant data, and any unauthorized access could result in material adverse effects.

Climate change and environmental regulations may adversely impact operations and increase costs. The Company faces increasing regulatory requirements related to environmental sustainability.

Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

The following discussion should be read in conjunction with the Company's consolidated financial statements.

Total net sales decreased 1% or $3.0 billion during 2020 compared to 2019. The weakness in net sales was driven primarily by lower iPhone sales, partially offset by higher Wearables, Home and Accessories and Services revenue. The Company believes its continued investment in research and development is critical to its future growth.

The Company's gross margin improved to 38.2% in 2020 compared to 37.8% in 2019, reflecting a favorable shift in product mix. Operating expenses as a percentage of net sales remained relatively stable.

The Company generated strong operating cash flow of $80.7 billion in fiscal 2020, an increase from $69.4 billion in fiscal 2019. This growth was primarily driven by higher net income and changes in working capital.

Results of Operations

Revenue:
The Company's total net revenues for fiscal year 2020 were $274.5 billion, compared to $260.2 billion in 2019.

Operating income grew to $66.3 billion from $63.9 billion in the prior year. Net income was $57.4 billion, an increase from $55.3 billion.

We expect revenue growth to continue in fiscal 2021, with guidance of approximately $290 billion.
